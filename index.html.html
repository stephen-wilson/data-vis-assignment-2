Subtheme: Housing for the Rich

Overall Analysis Questions

  1. Does building more luxury buildings create more affordable housing?
    My motivation behind this question is that throughout the reading “Boston Towers of Wealth”, one of the main justifications provided by the real estate developers was that building the luxury buildings actually created more affordable housing and opportunities. Walsh’s claim from the reading shows their perspective: “Oftentimes people will talk about luxury buildings, saying that, you know, we’re not building for the poor,” said Walsh at One Dalton’s topping-off ceremony in 2018. “But what this building actually does, it’s creating opportunities and revenue for middle-class and low-income housing in the city of Boston.”
  2. Is there a relationship between absentee buyers and the location of luxury housing?
    My motivation for this question was my curiosity as to whether there is a relationship between absentee buyers and luxury housing which could provide insight into if there is perhaps an area of Boston that is more ripe for speculation and if there is a correlation with the prices of the area around it.
  3. Is there a profit incentive for providing affordable housing or is it a net loss or always lower profit for real estate developers to do so?
    My motivation for this is that I saw in the reading that it says “Developers disproportionately chase profits at the highest end of the Boston market,” however, I was wondering whether the data suggests that it is always more profitable to build luxury buildings, or whether it would still be profitable and a reasonable business decision to invest in affordable housing as well.

Discoveries & Insights

Summary

In summary, the visualizations do not suggest that building more luxury housing causes more affordable housing to be built (as seen by the decrease in non-investor property purchases over time), there is a potential relationship between absentee buyers and the location since there are distinct zip codes in Boston where this claim could be made, and finally, there is an incentive for building more affordable housing because there is potential for high annual values from those properties to be had, so building more for more people will naturally lead to profit in the long-term.
